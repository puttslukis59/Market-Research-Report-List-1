<p><p>The automotive suspension device market includes several types of springs that enhance vehicle performance and ride comfort. Leaf springs, typically used in trucks and SUVs, provide load support and stability. Spiral springs, or coil springs, offer flexibility and are common in passenger cars. Hydro-pneumatic springs combine gas and fluid technology for adaptable suspension. Torsion bar springs use twisting motion to absorb shocks, ideal for heavy-duty applications. Other types may include air springs and active suspension systems, catering to diverse vehicle needs.</p></p>
