<p><p>The polymer banknote substrates market encompasses two primary types: polymer substrates and hybrid paper-polymer substrates. Polymer substrates are entirely made from polymer materials, offering durability, security features, and resistance to water and dirt, making them ideal for modern currency. In contrast, hybrid paper-polymer substrates combine traditional paper with polymer layers, aiming to enhance the note's lifespan and security while maintaining some characteristics of paper. Both types cater to the growing demand for secure and efficient currency solutions globally.</p></p>
