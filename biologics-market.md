<p><p>The biologics market encompasses several key types including monoclonal antibodies, vaccines, recombinant proteins, antisense therapies, and RNA interference (RNAi). Monoclonal antibodies target specific antigens for treating diseases like cancer and autoimmune disorders. Vaccines stimulate immune responses to prevent infections. Recombinant proteins, produced through genetic engineering, serve therapeutic functions, including hormone replacement. Antisense therapies inhibit the expression of disease-causing genes, while RNAi mechanisms silence specific RNA molecules to block protein synthesis, offering potential in gene-targeted therapies.</p></p>
