<p><p>The biosurfactants market is categorized primarily into three types: rhamnolipids, sophorolipids, and others. Rhamnolipids, produced by Pseudomonas aeruginosa, are known for their effective emulsifying and antimicrobial properties, making them valuable in agriculture and bioremediation. Sophorolipids, derived from yeast, exhibit excellent surface activity and biodegradability, making them suitable for personal care and cleaning products. The "others" category includes a variety of biosurfactants sourced from different microorganisms, catering to diverse applications across industries like food, pharmaceuticals, and cosmetics.</p></p>
